About London Time & Timezone

London, United Kingdom, uses the Europe/London timezone. During winter London is on GMT (Greenwich Mean Time, UTC+0). In summer it advances to BST (British Summer Time, UTC+1). The clocks change on the last Sunday of March (spring forward) and the last Sunday of October (fall back).

Common London Time Differences

London (GMT) vs New York (EST): +5 hours. London vs Los Angeles (PST): +8 hours. London vs Dubai (GST): -4 hours. London vs Sydney (AEDT): -11 hours. These offsets may shift by ±1 hour depending on daylight saving changes in each region.

Frequently Asked Questions

What time is it in London right now?

The live clock at the top of this page shows the exact current time in London. London observes GMT in winter and BST in summer.

Does London observe daylight saving time?

Yes. The UK observes British Summer Time (BST, UTC+1) from the last Sunday of March to the last Sunday of October. Outside this period, London runs on GMT (UTC+0).

What is the difference between GMT and BST?

GMT (Greenwich Mean Time) is UTC+0 and is used in winter. BST (British Summer Time) is UTC+1 and is used in summer. The timezone badge on the clock above always shows which one is currently active.
