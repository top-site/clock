About New York Time & Timezone

New York City, USA, uses the America/New_York timezone. During winter New York is on EST (Eastern Standard Time, UTC−5). In summer it advances to EDT (Eastern Daylight Time, UTC−4). The clocks change on the second Sunday of March (spring forward) and the first Sunday of November (fall back).

Common New York Time Differences

New York (EST) vs London (GMT): −5 hours. New York vs Los Angeles (PST): +3 hours. New York vs Dubai (GST): −9 hours. New York vs Sydney (AEDT): −16 hours. These offsets may shift by ±1 hour depending on daylight saving changes in each region.

Frequently Asked Questions

What time is it in New York right now?

The live clock at the top of this page shows the exact current time in New York. New York observes EST in winter and EDT in summer.

Does New York observe daylight saving time?

Yes. New York observes Eastern Daylight Time (EDT, UTC−4) from the second Sunday of March to the first Sunday of November. Outside this period, New York runs on EST (UTC−5).

What is the difference between EST and EDT?

EST (Eastern Standard Time) is UTC−5 and is used in winter. EDT (Eastern Daylight Time) is UTC−4 and is used in summer. The timezone badge on the clock above always shows which one is currently active.

What is the time difference between New York and Los Angeles?

New York (Eastern Time) is 3 hours ahead of Los Angeles (Pacific Time) year-round, since both cities observe daylight saving time simultaneously.
